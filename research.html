Research

Here's some of what I've been doing in my PhD

In my PhD thesis, I am currently trying to understand the diversity and evolutionary patterns of vocalizations in the brazilian genus Bokermannohyla, and how they correlate with the underlying anatomical apparatus which produce their calls. My work has two main approaches: 1) the characterization of the laryngeal anatomy (the phonatory organ of anurans) combining classical (gross dissection) and modern techniques (microcomputed tomography scan); and 2) the detailed and standardized characterization of the vocal repertoires of each Bokermannohyla species, looking for major acoustic patterns. The ultimate goal of my study is to evaluate, through Phylogenetic Comparative Methods, whether traits of laryngeal elements (i.e. volume and size of vocal chord) reflect shifts or conservatisms of advertisement call traits (i.e. dominant frequency) among Bokermannohyla species.
